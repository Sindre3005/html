Michael Jackson

Michael Jacklson was a famous singer who had his prime throughout the 80s.

Famous albums

Thriller (1982)

Thriller is so famous because of its major success. It's the best-selling album of all time, selling over 70 million copies worldwide. Its style was designed to reach everyone, and was a mix of Pop, Rock, Funk, and R&B.

Bad (1987)

Bad was a follow-up to thriller, and the first album to produce five no. 1 singles in the U.S. Its style included hints of more aggressive pop-rock, with social commentary.

Medical issues and illness

Michael Jackson suffered from a variety of different medical problems such as Vitiligo, Lupus, and other issues that don't really fit in as illness, but more as general health problems

Vitiligo

Vitiligo is a skin condition where patches of the skin lose their pigment. This made so his skin lightened throughout the 80-90s, and so he used depigmentation treatments to even out the tone.

Lupus

Lupus is an autoimmune disease where the immune system attacks healthy cells. Jackson apparently had a mild form of Lupus, which can cause skin sensitivity, scarring, and increase the risk of Vitiligo.

Other health issues

Jackson suffered second-degree burns on his scalp during the filming of a pepsi commercial, leading to a long process of scalp and hair treatments, and potentially contributing to his later painkiller dependency.

In later years of his life, Jackson suffered from insomnia and relied heavily on sedatives and painkilling meds. This dependency contributed to his later death from propofol intoxication.
